About the Project

The Project

The Ocean Health Dashboard is a personal project built out of a genuine interest in the ocean and what the data tells us about its evolution. The goal is simple: make 33 years of ocean science explorable by anyone, without needing a scientific background to understand what's happening.

Temperature, salinity and pH are three of the most telling indicators of ocean health. Watching them shift across decades — year by year, season by season — is a sobering reminder of how much the ocean has already changed, and how much it still absorbs on our behalf.

Data Sources
  • Copernicus Marine Service (CMEMS): GLORYS12 physics reanalysis (temperature, salinity)
  • CMEMS PISCES BGC: Biogeochemical model reanalysis (ocean pH)
  • NASA Aquarius / ESA SMOS: Satellite salinity cross-validation
  • SOCAT v2023 / GOA-ON: Surface ocean CO₂ and pH observation network
  • Argo Float Network: In-situ BGC profiling floats

A personal project, an interactive climate visualization

Explore 33 years of ocean data across three key indicators: sea surface temperature, salinity and pH, spanning 1993 to 2025.

Navigate through time with the timeline, compare any two years side by side, and click anywhere on the ocean to pin a reading that stays live as you explore.
