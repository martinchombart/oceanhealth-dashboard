The ocean breathes through its surface. Oxygen dissolves from the atmosphere and from phytoplankton photosynthesis, then circulates downward as currents mix the water column. Everything below the surface depends on this slow ventilation.

It is now slowing down. Warmer water holds less dissolved oxygen, a basic fact of chemistry, and a warmer surface layer also blocks the mixing that brings oxygen to deeper waters. The global ocean has lost about 2% of its oxygen since 1960, and "Oxygen Minimum Zones" already cover nearly 8% of the ocean by volume.

Coastal areas face a second pressure: agricultural runoff feeds plankton blooms that suffocate the water as they decompose. More than 700 coastal "dead zones" now exist worldwide, the largest covering thousands of square kilometers each summer. Water that, for most marine life, is simply unbreathable.
